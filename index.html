About the Artist

Where he’s from, where he is, and where he’s going

Born in 1977, Ashish K. Saxena spent his early childhood growing up in Calcutta. In 1999, he moved to Iowa, United States to pursue a college degree. The vast landscarpes of rolling hills and perfect blue skies in the Midwest presented a stark contrast to the busy streets of Calcultta. As a college student, he was exposed to and built an appreciation for the monumental murals by Grant Wood, Diego River, as well as artists such as Jackson Pollock, Frank Klein,and Robert Motherwell. This exposure combined with his education in communications, semiotics, linguistics, and Tibettan Buddhist philosophy led him to finally break away from figurative art in favor of abstract expressionism.

The artist currently resides in the UAE and continues to explore the crossroads of arts and development where meaning and experience are reconciled. Deeply influenced by the works of Pollock, the artist pays hommage to one of the most iconic painters of the 20th century and takes conversation further by exploring 'action painting' on large canvases that fill the visual field.
