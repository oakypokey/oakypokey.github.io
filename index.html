About the Artist

Where he’s from, where he is, and where he’s going

Born in 1977, Ashish K. Saxena spent his early childhood growing up in Calcutta and in 1999 moved to the United States where he graduated from the University of Iowa with a BA in Advertising and a BA in International Business, followed by an MSc in Journalism and Mass Communications.

The vast landscapes of rolling hills and perfect blue skies in the Midwest presented a stark contrast to the busy streets of Calcutta.

As a college student, he was exposed to and built an appreciation for the monumental murals by Grant Wood, Diego Rivera, as well as artists such as Jackson Pollock, Franz Klein, and Robert Motherwell. This exposure combined with his education in communications, semiotics, linguistics, and Tibetan Buddhist philosophy led him to finally break away from figurative art in favour of abstract expressionism.

The artist currently resides in the UAE and continues to explore the crossroads of arts and development where meaning and experience are reconciled. Deeply influenced by the works of Pollock, the artist pays homage to one of the most iconic painters of the 20th century and takes conversation further by exploring 'action painting' on large canvases that fill the visual field.

“In a deeply fragmented world, with each fragment vying for attention, we often forget the rich tapestry that makes the whole is more important than any one of the parts. Hopefully, these paintings communicate the importance of social and ecological balance and equanimity”

Inspired by the Abstract Expressionist art movement that originated in New York in the 1950s and 60s, Ashish seeks to reintroduces the fundamental ideas behind the art movement into the fractured nature of contemporary art today.

In a world filled with visual clutter and pollution, clever hooks and intrusive suggestions, the artist’s work liberates lines and colors from time, from location and intent, and explores the connection between visible form and the mind of the viewer. The question is no longer what the artist is trying to communicate, but what is the viewers evocative response to what is presented.

Large gestures and rhythmic movements that reflect a process, an immediacy and a tremendous sense of force - Each piece pushing the boundaries - both physical and meta-physical, enveloping the observer in a milieu of rich fractal patterns and emotional ambiguity. The paintings engage the viewer and stimulate their creative processes.

Using ‘action painting’, a style popularized by Jackson Pollock, the artist paints from the sub–conscious—with great intensity, speed, and fearlessness – layering color on color, a visual smorgasbord of call-and-response – there is no accident, no mistake, no beginning, no end.

Ashish is a self-taught artist and has been deeply influenced by sand painting tradition of the native Americans that he studied for six years in the American Midwest, as well as by the mandala art of Tibetan Buddhists that is his current muse.
